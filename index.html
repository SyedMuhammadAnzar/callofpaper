The outbreak of the Covid-19 pandemic has severely affected the economy, society, health, education, and governance in both the developed and developing countries. Covid-19 has affected not just key industries globally, but also has caused sociological and socioeconomic implications. The lives of individuals, households, and business entities have been disrupted due to continuous lockdowns and international border closings. According to the IMF's April 2021 World Economic Outlook Report, the global economy contracted by 3.5 percent in 2020, a 7 percent drop from 3.4 percent growth anticipated in October 2019 (IMF, 2020). A comparison of data for April 2020 vs December 2019 reveals that industrial production fell globally by 20 percent on an average (UNIDO, 2020).
Additionally, there were unprecedented global employment losses of 114 million jobs in 2020 relative to 2019. Moreover, the employment losses were higher for women (5.0 per cent) than for men and for young workers (8.7 per cent) than for older workers (ILO, 2021). In monetary terms, the global labor income fell by an estimated US$3.7 trillion (using 2019 market exchange rates) in 2020 relative to 2019. This corresponds to 4.4 per cent of global GDP in 2019 (ILO, 2021). Global economy could lose over $4 trillion due to Covid-19 impact on tourism. Developing countries have borne the biggest brunt of the pandemic’s impact on tourism. They suffered the largest reductions in tourist arrivals in 2020, estimated at between 60% and 80% (UNCTAD, 2021). The effect of Covid-19 proved a double-edged sword to the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries. They were hit by not only the crisis of the pandemic but also decline in oil prices, both of which led to drastic fall in their revenues and negatively affected their position in the global financial market.
This multi-disciplinary conference aims to bring together scientists, researchers, and policymakers from different segments of economy, social sciences, and humanities for discussion and debate on the implications of the Covid-19 pandemic to the economy and society at large. Furthermore, this event would assess the effectiveness of the measures undertaken by the stakeholders to mitigate the overall impact of the pandemic. This conference will serve as an international platform for exchanging views, insights, and experiences of Covid-19 across the industries and the world.

Topics of Interest

Participants are invited to contribute to this interdisciplinary conference platform by submitting their papers in fields that cover the theoretical and empirical studies, as well industrial experiences. Submitted papers should demonstrate significant advances on the impact of the Covid-19 pandemic in the following areas:

Impact of the Covid-19 Pandemic on the Economy

  • The effect on the economy at macro levels.
  • The effect on the economy at micro levels.
  • The effect on economic sector and industries.
  • Diversification and sustainable development.
  • Economic of epidemiology.
  • Economics of the firm.

Impact of the Covid-19 Pandemic on Financial Policies, Institutions, and Markets

  • The effect on the monetary policies
  • The effect on the fiscal policies in Markets
  • The effect on financial services
  • The effect on stock markets
  • The effect on the banking sector
  • The effect on financial technology

Impact of the Covid-19 Pandemic on the Business Sector

  • Private sector enterprises including SMEs and MSMEs
  • Electronic commerce in Business sector
  • Innovation and entrepreneurship
  • Construction/real estate
  • Manufacturing and non-manufacturing sectors
  • Hospitality (hotels and tourism)

Impact of the Covid-19 Pandemic on International Trade and Logistics

  • International trade
  • Logistics and global value chains
  • Global cargo and freight flows
  • Container availability and customs clearance
  • Logistics and global value chains automation
  • Acceleration of Industrial Revolution 4.0 technology

Impact of the Covid-19 Pandemic on the Society

  • Labor market, national workforce, and migrant workers
  • Educational sector
  • Vaccinations and healthcare
  • Sports and leisure activities
  • Households and consumption
  • Social interactions and behaviors

Impact of the Covid-19 Pandemic on the Energy Market

  • Economics of energy
  • Hydrocarbons and non-hydrocarbons
  • Diversification of energy
  • The rise of green biofuel
  • Renewable sources of energy
  • Digitization and use of online platforms

Public Policy and Global Responses to the Covid-19 Pandemic

  • Public health policy in Covid-19 Pandemic
  • Environment policy in Covid-19 Pandemic
  • Public finance
  • Governance
  • Nationalization of labor market
  • Global best practices

Manuscript

The participants are invited to submit their abstracts through the conference Submission System before 15 May 2022, and their full papers before 15 of August 2022. Submissions must be original and should not have been published previously or be under consideration for publication while being evaluated for this conference. The proceedings of the conference will be published by Palgrave Macmillan.

How To Submit Your Abstract

1-To submit your abstract, go to the Submission System.

2-Create your account. Your email address will be used as your username, and you will be asked to create a password. Please be aware that the information you enter when creating your account will be used for official Letters of Acceptance, Letters of Invitation, and Conference Programmes, so please ensure correct spelling of names, affiliated institutions, and so forth.

3-Submitted abstract must be no more than 250 words including the aim of the study, methodology, significance, and contribution and expected findings.

4-Participants can benefit from Early Bird or Advanced Registration rates before 30 June 2022.

5-Submitted abstract will undergo double-blind peer review and notification of acceptance will be sent generally within two weeks.

6-Upon payment of the registration fee, you will receive a confirmation email containing your official receipt.

Submission Status

1-Check on the status of your abstract submission by logging in to the online submission system. The status is displayed in the "Your Submissions" area.

2-If your paper is accepted for presentation at the conference, a notification email will be sent to your registered email address. If you do not receive this email, please contact the conference secretariat at covid-19@squ.edu.om

Conference Proceedings

1-All submitted papers that meet the standard for publication will be published in the conference proceedings. Final papers must be submitted in Microsoft Word format following the Author Guidelines  and formatted your paper accordingly.
